The City Bleeds

Poem | September 2024

The Text

The street is a wet slate,
reflecting a tired neon sigh.
Rain washes the grit from the concrete,
but not the years from the eye.

Another siren fades out on the edge,
a promise the city keeps breaking.
We stand on the crumbling window ledge,
and watch what the dark is taking.
