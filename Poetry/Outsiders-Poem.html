Traveller

Poem | September 2024

The Text


I travel down a track by fate unfound,
my late song loud upon the blackened stone.
O Sleep grotesque, in skin and leather bound,
this keep of lies forlorn but marked alone.
Embarking might defy this gothic soul,
in darkest night a bane ungainly born,
when all the light had fled his eyes now cold,
alas the fight again doth tell my scorn.

O moon so bright yet dances bold in lake,
a face is seen and met when mad men look,
embraced by what these rippled shadows make,
as lips do whisper sweetly lies we took.
Abyss to fore of mind when fell from shore,
ask not of this when death doth tell us more.
